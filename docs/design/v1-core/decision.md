# Decide — chosen direction

*Design skill, stage 3. Chosen by Freddi 2026-07-12.*

## Chosen: Paste-front, Cookbook-home (the A/B fusion)

Home opens on the **capture magic** — a paste bar as hero — but the body of
the screen is the **growing photo shelf** (Concept A's library). One screen
serves both halves of Romy's pain: week-one capture *and* long-haul browsing.
It leads with the app's loudest verb (*paste*) without sacrificing the
browsable collection that beats her wall of Notes.

**Signature moment (carried from B):** paste → the bar morphs in place into a
live extraction (skeleton → the recipe fills itself in). The anti-screenshot
moment, made literal.

## Killed — with reasons

- **A — pure Cookbook.** Right about the library, wrong about the front door.
  It buries capture (the *loud* pain) one tap down, and opens to an empty shelf
  on day one. The fusion keeps A's library as the home *body* while leading
  with capture — so A isn't so much killed as absorbed.

- **B — pure Paste Bar.** Right instinct on the hero, but a paste bar over a
  plain *recent-list* never gives Romy the photo-rich, browsable collection
  that makes leaving Notes feel like an upgrade. The fusion keeps B's hero
  paste and swaps the list underneath for the shelf.

- **C — This Week / cook-first.** Answers a question Romy isn't asking yet
  ("what should I cook?") and edges into meal-planning, which is explicitly out
  of V1 scope. **Cook-mode is banked as a V2 idea** — a strong one — not led
  with now.

## Stays killed

No quiet revivals of C's home posture in V1. Cook-mode returns only as an
explicit, scoped V2 conversation.
